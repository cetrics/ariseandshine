Peanut Butter Processing

Peanut butter processing at Arise and Shine CBO is more than just a vocational skill—it is a lifeline for caregivers of children with disabilities. In a region where economic challenges often prevent families from accessing quality care and resources, this program provides a sustainable source of income while allowing caregivers to remain present in their children’s lives. Many of these children require consistent attention, therapy, and special accommodations, which makes formal employment difficult for their guardians. By training caregivers in peanut butter production using locally sourced peanuts, Arise and Shine equips them to produce, package, and sell a nutritious product that is already in high demand within local markets.

The impact of this initiative extends far beyond income generation. It fosters independence, dignity, and self-worth among caregivers who may have once felt isolated or powerless. Participating in peanut butter production gives them not only a skill but also a renewed sense of purpose. The training sessions are designed to be inclusive and empowering, bringing together caregivers from diverse backgrounds to learn, share, and support one another. This sense of community reduces the emotional burden of caregiving and helps erase the stigma often associated with raising children who have special needs. In turn, children benefit directly from better nutrition, improved home environments, and a more emotionally supported caregiver.

Looking ahead, Arise and Shine CBO envisions scaling this program into a fully operational peanut butter manufacturing company. This expansion will open doors to employment for even more caregivers while creating a steady revenue stream to fund additional therapy, education, and disability support services. The initiative is a perfect example of the organization's core values—empowerment, inclusivity, and community participation—translated into action. By investing in peanut butter processing, Arise and Shine is not only lifting individual families but also nourishing a culture of resilience, enterprise, and hope for children with disabilities across Kericho and beyond.
