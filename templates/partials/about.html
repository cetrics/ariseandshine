Welcome to Arise & Shine

Arise and Shine CBO is a certified, community-based organization located in Kericho, Kenya, dedicated to supporting children with disabilities and neurodevelopmental disorders. Founded in 2023 and officially registered in 2024, our mission is to advocate for the rights, dignity, and inclusion of these individuals while empowering caregivers and families. We provide a range of services including inclusive care, vocational training, special education, and occupational therapy. Through a team of dedicated professionals and trained volunteers, we strive to build a more understanding, inclusive society where every child can thrive.

We also run advocacy and awareness campaigns to combat stigma and promote acceptance, while offering certified programs focused on practical support and long-term sustainability. Our efforts are strengthened through partnerships with local government bodies, NGOs, faith-based institutions, and development partners. At Arise and Shine CBO, we believe that every individual—regardless of ability—deserves equal opportunities, access to resources, and the chance to live a fulfilling life.

Our Focus

Vision

A society where every individual, regardless of ability, has equal opportunities, access to resources, and the support needed to thrive.

Mission

To champion for the rights, dignity and inclusion of individuals with disabilities and neurodevelopmental disorders through advocacy, education and community support.

Core Values

  • Empowerment: Building capacity for long-term self-reliance.
  • Integrity: Transparency and honesty in all operations.
  • Inclusivity: Equal opportunities for all, regardless of background.
  • Accountability: Responsible stewardship of resources.
  • Community Participation: Collaborative engagement for sustainable change.

Objectives

  • Conduct outreach and awareness campaigns to reduce stigma and promote inclusion.
  • Advocate for policy change/enforcement to protect and promote disability rights.
  • Improve economic independence of caregivers through skills development training.
  • Enhance care for beneficiaries with neurodevelopmental disorders.
  • Increase social inclusion by empowering caregivers and promoting awareness.
